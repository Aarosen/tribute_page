Gordon Moore

Life and accomplishments from a world renounced entrepreneur/engineer

The most important moments in his life:

  • 1929 - Born in San Francisco, CA
  • 1950 - Obtained his Bachelor's degree in Chemistry from UC Berkeley
  • 1954 - Obtained his Ph.D in Physics from Caltech
  • 1956 - United with William Shockley in Shockley Semiconductor Laboratory
  • 1957 - Joined Fairchild Semiconductor Corporation
  • 1965 - More was asked to predict developments over the next decade in technology. Now known as "Moore's Law"
  • 1968 - Formed Intel Corporation with Robert Noyce
  • 1990 - Awarded National Medal of Technology
  • 1993 - Awarded John Fritz Medal
  • 1993 - Became chairman of the board of trustees of Caltech
  • 1997 - Awarded IEEE Founders Medal
  • 1998 - Appointed Computer History Museum Fellow
  • 2001 - Awarded Othmer Gold Medal
  • 2002 - Awarded Presidential Medal of Freedom
  • 2004 - Awarded Perkin Medal
  • 2006 - Awarded Nierenberg Price
  • 2008 - Awarded IEEE Medal of Honor

"No physical quantity can continue to change exponentially forever. Your job is delaying forever." Gordon Moore
